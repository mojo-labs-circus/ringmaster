# Family Docs — Handover

Three documents to write. All templates — rewrite in your own voice, add the jokes.


## 1. Group chat message

Short, casual message to the family group chat. Cover:

- What it is: AI assistant you built for the family, like Claude but runs on a home server you're building

- Privacy angle: data stays in the house, not on someone else's servers — keep it simple, don't over-explain

- What it does: chat, tasks, web search, documents, research, remembers context over time, personalised per person

- Each person gets their own assistant with their own name and personalisation  

- Timeline: end of summer 2026

- Heads up that a short form is coming so you can collect what everyone actually wants

- Heads up that individual messages are coming for each person

- Basically JARVIS from Iron Man

- Learns over time


## 2. Personalised "what this will do for you" messages

One per person. Short — half a page each. Written like a personal note from you to them. Frame it around what they actually do, not a feature list.

**Charlie** (brother, UK, Power tier)

- Fintech analysis, client tracking, light coding

- Angles: research and data summaries, help building his client tracking tools, drafting reports, remembers project context across sessions

- He's technical enough to get more detail

**Grace** (sister 1, UK, Standard tier)

- Insurance work, documents, spreadsheets

- Angles: drafts and edits documents fast, summarises long reports, handles the repetitive writing bits of her job, spreadsheet help

- Keep it practical, no jargon

**Lilly** (sister 2, UK, Standard tier)

- PhD research, dissertation writing, long document analysis

- Angles: reads and summarises papers, helps structure arguments and sections, writing feedback, tracks where she left off across sessions — basically a research assistant that doesn't forget

- Will be insanely useful for her if she learns how to use it (but luckily its not difficult to use at all and I will be able to adapt it to however she finds easiest)

**Mom** (US, Standard tier)

- Interior design research, creative queries

- Angles: research and inspiration gathering, style and material lookups, helping plan and describe ideas, a creative sounding board that remembers her taste, helping plan work trips

- Keep it warm and fun

**Dad** (US, Standard tier)

- Spreadsheets, documents, family admin, general productivity

- Angles: spreadsheet formulas, drafting and editing documents, answering questions without having to google, keeps a task list, stores passwords, FINALLY HIS OWN SYSTEM! Will help remove the things that he finds a pain in the butt to do.

- Practical and no-nonsense, he won't want a long read


## 3. Intake form

Paste these questions into Google Forms or Microsoft Forms. One form, send the same link to everyone.

**Title:** Help me build your AI assistant

**Intro text:** I'm building a personal AI assistant for each of us and I want to make sure it's actually useful to you — not just what I think you'd want. Takes about 5 minutes. No right or wrong answers, just be honest.

**Questions:**

1. What do you do in your daily life that you find repetitive and annoying — stuff you'd rather not have to deal with? *(paragraph)*

2. What do you find yourself googling a lot? *(paragraph)*

3. What kinds of documents do you write or work with regularly? *(paragraph)*

4. Are there any routines or tasks in your week you'd love to automate or just hand off? *(paragraph)*

5. If you had a personal assistant available any time, what would you use them for most? *(paragraph)*

6. Is there anything specific you'd want your AI to know about you or how you like things done? *(paragraph)*

7. do you use any AI-s already. If so what do you like / not like about them? 

8. Anything else you want to add? *(paragraph, optional)*
